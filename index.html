Rules
The offside rule is a crucial aspect of football that aims to prevent players from gaining an unfair advantage by positioning themselves ahead of the last defender when the ball is played to them. A player is deemed offside if they are closer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-to-last defender when the ball is passed to them.
The handball rule states that players are not allowed to deliberately handle or control the ball with their hands or arms, except for the goalkeeper within their penalty area. If a player handles the ball in an intentional or unnatural manner, it results in a free-kick or penalty kick, depending on the location of the offense.
Foul and Free-Kick Rule: Fouls occur when a player makes illegal contact with an opponent, such as tripping, pushing, or using excessive force. Depending on the severity and location of the foul, the opposing team is awarded a free-kick, either direct or indirect. Direct free-kicks allow the team to score directly from the kick, while indirect free-kicks require the ball to touch another player before a goal can be scored.
